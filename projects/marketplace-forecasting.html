Marketplace Revenue & Unit Sold Forecasting

Multi-Method Time-Series Analysis for Business Intelligence

Project Overview

This project focuses on developing a robust, multi-method time-series model to forecast daily revenue for various marketplaces. The primary goal was to provide data-driven predictions for short-term revenue (a 3-day forecast) to support operational planning, inventory management, and strategic financial decision-making for each unique marketplace.

Technologies & Methods

Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Time-Series Analysis Moving Average Exponential Smoothing Linear Regression

Forecasting Models Applied

  • Moving Average (MA): A 7-day rolling average to smooth short-term fluctuations and capture weekly trends
  • Exponential Smoothing (ESF): Applies exponentially decreasing weights to older observations with smoothing factor (Î±) of 0.7
  • Linear Regression Forecasting (LRF): A linear trendline fitted to historical daily revenue over time

Data Preprocessing

  • Data Source: Historical order data from data_orders.xlsx
  • Aggregation: Raw data cleaned and aggregated by order_date to calculate Daily Revenue and Daily Units Sold
  • Segmentation: Analysis performed separately for each unique marketplace to ensure highest predictive accuracy

Comparative Analysis Results

The project delivered a comprehensive, comparative forecasting tool, illustrating the trade-offs between models:

  • Moving Average provided the smoothest curve, effectively filtering out noise but lagging behind sharp changes
  • Exponential Smoothing was the most responsive to recent changes due to the higher smoothing factor
  • Linear Regression offered a clear long-term direction, useful for setting overall expectations

The final output included concrete 3-day revenue forecasts for every unique marketplace, providing immediate and actionable insights for business stakeholders.
